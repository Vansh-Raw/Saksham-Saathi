Preventing and Reporting Cyber Crime

Cyber crime is a growing concern in today's digital age. It can take many forms, such as hacking, identity theft, and online fraud. At Saksham Saathi, we provide resources and guidance on how to protect yourself and your loved ones from cyber crime and what to do if you become a victim.

How to Report Cyber Crime

If you are a victim of cybercrime, you should report it to the appropriate authorities as soon as possible. Here are some steps you can follow:

  1. Gather evidence: Take screenshots, save emails, and collect any other relevant information about the crime.
  2. File a complaint: You can file a complaint with the local police or with a federal agency, depending on the type of crime. Here are some websites where you can file a complaint:
    • Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3): File a complaint related to internet crime, such as phishing, hacking, or online fraud.
    • Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI): Contact your local FBI field office to report cybercrime, such as cyberstalking, online threats, or child exploitation.
    • Computer Crime and Intellectual Property Section (CCIPS): Report computer and intellectual property crimes, such as copyright infringement, trademark fraud, or trade secret theft.
  3. Follow up: After you file a complaint, you may be contacted by law enforcement for further information or to provide additional evidence. Be sure to follow up and cooperate with the authorities to help them investigate and prosecute the crime.
